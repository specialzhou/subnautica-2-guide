Subnautica 2 crafting is the engine that drives every stage of your survival. Unlike most survival games, there are no quest markers pointing you to the next recipe — you discover blueprints by scanning objects, exploring wrecks, and reading PDA entries. This guide compiles every verified recipe in the Early Access version (as of May 2026), with exact material counts and where to find each ingredient.

The crafting system revolves around five main stations: the Fabricator (instant crafting of tools and consumables), the Processor (refines ingots and advanced materials with real-time delay), the Modification Station (upgrade modules), the Habitat Builder (base construction), and the Vehicle Fabricator (builds the Tadpole submarine).

How Crafting Works in Subnautica 2

Subnautica 2's crafting system is blueprint-gated: you must first unlock a recipe before you can craft it. Blueprints unlock through three main methods:

  1. Scanning fragments of an item with your Scanner tool — usually requires 2–4 fragments of the same object
  2. Reading certain PDA entries, which automatically grant related recipes
  3. Exploring specific biomes or data terminals that auto-unlock advanced blueprints

Once a blueprint is unlocked, open the appropriate crafting station and select the item. Materials are consumed from your inventory. The Fabricator crafts instantly. The Processor has a real-time delay — approximately 30 seconds for a Titanium Ingot and up to 2 minutes for complex items.

⚠️ Lifepod Fabricator Limitation
The Fabricator built into your starting lifepod is intentionally limited. Many recipes will show a "Requires Fully Functional Fabricator" error. You must build a new Fabricator inside a proper base to access the full recipe pool. This is one of the most common sources of new-player confusion.
💡 Pro Tip
Always carry your Scanner. Every piece of wreckage, every creature, every plant can be scanned — and many scans directly unlock new recipes. The Scanner is one of the highest-ROI tools in the game.

📌 Where to Find Copper
Copper comes from two sources: limestone outcrops (grey rock formations broken with your Survival Multitool) and glowing cave wall/ceiling nodes. The cave directly beneath your lifepod is the best early-game spot for Copper — look for glowing orange/yellow nodes on the walls.

Plasteel Ingots are crafted in the Processor from Titanium ×2 + Lithium ×1. They gate the High Capacity Air Tank and other mid-game recipes. Lithium is found in limestone outcrops alongside Titanium and Copper — always collect it when you see it.

Crafting Tips & Tricks

Build a Base Fabricator Early

The lifepod Fabricator is intentionally limited — you will hit the "Requires Fully Functional Fabricator" error constantly until you build a base with a proper Fabricator installed. Prioritize getting a base with a real Fabricator as soon as possible. The Habitat Builder recipe requires: Titanium ×2 + Glass ×1 + Basic Battery ×1 + Copper Wire ×1.

Processor Timing — Queue Smart

The Processor has real-time delays (~30 seconds per Titanium Ingot, up to 2 minutes for complex items). Start processing runs before you go out to collect more materials — let it run while you dive. Never stand and wait for it.

Upgrade in Order — Tank Chain Never Skips

The oxygen tank upgrade line is a consuming chain: Standard Air Tank → High Capacity Air Tank → Ultra High Capacity Air Tank. Each step consumes the previous tank. You cannot skip tiers. Budget for this when planning material collection.

💡 Quartz vs Outcrops
Many beginners spend time breaking outcrops looking for Quartz and find none. That's because Quartz does not come from outcrops — it spawns as freestanding white/semi-transparent crystal clusters that you simply pick up by hand. Look for them on sand and cave walls.

Batch Your Resource Runs

Before a resource run, plan exactly what you need. Count required quantities for all your planned recipes, add a 20% buffer, and collect everything in one dive. Context-switching between crafting and diving is the biggest time sink for beginners.

⚠️ Don't Over-Craft Early
It's tempting to craft every tool you can unlock, but your inventory is limited. Focus on the absolute priority items first (Survival Multitool, Scanner, Flashlight, Standard Air Tank, Habitat Builder) and only expand your toolkit once you have a base with storage.
